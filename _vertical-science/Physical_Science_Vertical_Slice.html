Scientific Tools and Measurement Vocabulary

Measurement

Measurement

The act or process of measuring something. A size, length, or amount known by measuring something.

Unit

Unit

particular amount of length, time, money, etc., that is used as a standard for counting or measuring

  • Feet and meters are units of length.
  • units of measurement
  • The dollar is the principal unit of American currency.

Volume

Volume

The amount of space, measured in cubic units, that an object or substance occupies. The box has a volume of three cubic meters.

Mass

Mass

Often defined as the amount of matter in an object.

English System

English System

The English system of measures is a collection of measures for length, volume, weight, area, etc. that have their roots in hundreds of years of history. They were standardized somewhat by the British Weights and Measures Act of 1824, which defined the Imperial Units to be used throughout the British Empire.

SI System

SI System

The International System of Units (SI, abbreviated from the French Système international (d'unités)) is the modern form of the metric system, and is the most widely used system of measurement.

Metric System

Metric System

The decimal measuring system based on the meter, liter, and gram as units of length, capacity, and weight or mass.

Liter

Liter

a metric unit of capacity, formerly defined as the volume of 1 kilogram of water under standard conditions, now equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters (about 1.75 pints).

Gallon

Gallon

a unit of volume for liquid measure equal to four quarts, in particular.

Kilogram

Kilogram

the SI unit of mass, equivalent to the international standard kept at Sèvres near Paris (approximately 2.205 lb).

Gram

Gram

a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram.

Pound

Pound

any of various units of mass and weight; specifically : a unit now in general use among English-speaking peoples equal to 16 avoirdupois ounces or 7000 grains or 0.4536 kilogram

2. Triple Beam Balance - Mass
Instruction Vocabulary Video Self-Check

What is Mass?

Mass is:
  1. A measure of how much matter or "stuff" is in an object.
  2. Not the same as weight. Weight is a force.

Triple Beam Balance

You measure mass using a triple beam balance (TBB).
  1. The unit is grams.
  2. It has grooves for riders to sit in at each labeled mass. Do not put riders between grooves.

Steps to using a Triple Beam Balance:

  1. Move all riders to far left groove.
  2. Pointer should be at zero. If not, turn the adjustment knob.
  3. Place object on tray.
  4. Start with the largest rider. Slide the rider to the right into each groove until the pointer hits the bottom, then slide the rider back on groove.
  5. Repeat for the next largest rider until you finish all three riders.
  6. The pointer should be pointing at zero with all 3 riders in position.
  7. Add the mass shown on each beam to get the total mass of the object.

3. Graduated Cylinder - Volume
Instruction Vocabulary Video Self-Check

What Is Volume?

Volume is the amount of liquid that you have. You measure volume with a graduated cylinder.

Graduated Cylinder

A graduated cylinder is a standard piece of laboratory glassware used to measure the volume of an object or amount of liquid. Do not use a beaker to measure the volume of a liquid.

  • The green checkmark represents the graduated cylinder.
  • The red x represents the beaker.

Characteristics of a Graduated Cylinder

  1. Tall cylinder made of glass that can hold liquid.
  2. Has measuring marks down the side of the glass.

Uses of a Graduated Cylinder

  1. Any situation where you need accurate volume measurements.
  2. Can also be used to calculate density.

Steps to using a graduated cylinder:

  1. Choose a graduated clinder that is the appropriate size for your situation.
    1. Too big and your measurement won’t be accurate enough.
    2. Too small and you won’t be able to fit the fluid inside the graduated cylinder.
  2. Make sure the graduated cylinder is clean and dry.
  3. Carefully pour liquid into graduated cylinder.
  4. Bend down to eye level of the top of the liquid. .
  5. Take the reading at the lowest part of the lens-like meniscus.

Accuracy

Volume

Definition

A measure of the space occupied by a sample of matter.

Liter

Definition

Metric unit for measuring volume.

Density

Definition

A measure of mass per unit of volume. The average density of an object equals its total mass divided by its total volume. An object made from a comparatively dense material (such as iron) will have less volume than an object of equal mass made from some less dense substance (such as water).
